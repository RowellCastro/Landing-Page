REWRITING MY STORY: FROM EMPTY PAGE TO FULFILLING CHAPTERS

Childhood Years

August 6th, 2004, marked my birth in this world.1 Born in Manila, specifically in Tondo.2 My family of four consisting of me, my brother, and our loving parents, shared a small but lively home.3 My dreams of being a policeman or firefighter swirled in my head but it was just for fun.4 It is because I was hoping for a successful future.5 Then, at the age of four, a rough incident with my bike led to a fractured left elbow and a four-month recovery period.6 My experience back then was pretty hard.7 Movement was limited because I did not want to experience this kind of event.8 After that, I became a very cautious person.9 My grandfather's affectionate nickname, "Owang", was given to me throughout childhood.10 sounds funny but it was a constant reminder of his fondness.11 Joining the world of education, I finished my kindergarten in our barangay’s daycare.12 Moving to the elementary phase, the busy halls of San Rafael Parochial School (SRPS) became my second home.13 It was filled with the happy experiences of my classmates and the thrill of learning.14 But when the school bell finally rang, my true joy lay in the simple pleasures of outdoor games with my neighbors.15 The quiet satisfaction of building elaborate Lego creations as well as the hobby of collecting its limited editions were also my favorite activities.16 Before, I did not take my studies seriously because I was not still aware of their importance.17 My parents' constant refrain, "Take your studies sincerely for a better future" resonated deeply.18 It shaped me into a responsible student.19 It was not just about grades, but about understanding the value of dedication and hard work – lessons that continue to guide me today.20

Teenage Years

Growing taller and feeling awkward – that is how my teenage years began.1 School crushes made me blush, and being shy made things even tougher.2 Online games became my go-to for fun and enjoyment during my free time.3 To be specific, I enjoyed playing block games because it powers my imagination.4 On the other hand, there are bad choices: hanging out with friends who smoked and drank.5 Sneaking out for late-night hangouts made me feel excited.6 By the time I got caught by my parents, it was a wake-up call.7 Dad always said, "Focus on studies", and his words hit me hard.8 I knew I could do better.9 Winning "Best in ICT" in grade 10 boosted my confidence.10 The teenage years were a mix of fun and trouble, like a wild ride.11 But school was important, and that is what I focused on 12 After finishing junior high school, I moved to my senior years.13 Sadly, the pandemic happened and I will finish my senior year online.14 Choosing the STEM strand felt right, thanks to my love for technology.15 This was the moment when I found the University of the East (UE).16 I spent my two years remaining years of high school in UE while only at online set-up.17 Thankfully I graduated senior high with honors.18 This phase taught me important lessons: be responsible, value education, and choose good friends who support you.19 Maybe I picked some wrong friends, but I learned from it.20 My teenage years were not perfect, but they made me who I am.21 Growing up is tough, but I will always be ready for what is next!22

College Years

My first college steps were hesitant.1 A mix of nerves and excitement under the new chapter.2 Daily commutes tested my patience.3 But yet the journey brought a certain satisfaction to my life.4 Joining COMSSO, one of my college’s organizations, injected social warmth into the mix.5 Attending the webinars was full of fun and recreation.6 Drawn to technology, I chose Information Technology.7 Aiming to carve my future in the tech world.8 The subject of History felt like a constant uphill battle in my curriculum.9 In simple words, it was hard.10 But subjects like Programming sparked a joy of creation and connection.11 My circle of friends blossomed unexpectedly during a group activity.12 Leading to shared laughter and explorations beyond campus walls.13 Adjusting from online classes to face-to-face was a struggle, but I learned to adapt and find new routines.14 After finishing my first year, a 1.50 GWA stood as a testament to my quiet perseverance.15 I was so proud of myself because I survived it.16 I hope this will be a consistent process in my career.17 College life, so far, is a quiet melody, one I prefer in the peace of solitude.18 Yet, I actively hone my communication skills, preparing for future interactions.19 The overall journey feels smooth and manageable.20 I am clinging to the hope of reaching graduation with unwavering dedication.21 This experience has instilled a powerful lesson – you will never learn in deceit.22
